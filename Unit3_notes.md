## 📌 1. BHAKTI MOVEMENT — Love & Devotion Revolution

> 🔴 Tier A | Frequency: 4/4 | Section: A + B + C ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT

### Definition:

**Bhakti Movement** (6th–17th century CE) ek powerful religious and social reform movement tha jo **pure devotion (bhakti) through God** ko emphasize karta tha — bina caste discrimination, bina rituals, bina Sanskrit knowledge ke. Koi bhi, kisi bhi caste ka insaan God ko directly worship kar sakta tha.

### Why Did Bhakti Movement Start?

1. **Rigid caste system** — lower castes ko temple mein entry nahi
2. **Brahmin domination** — complex rituals, only they could perform
3. **Social inequality** — untouchability widespread
4. **Need for simpler religion** — common people Sanskrit nahi jaante the
5. **Islamic influence** — Sufi movement ka parallel impact

### Key Features of Bhakti Movement:

| Feature                 | Explanation                                             |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Personal God**        | Direct relationship with God — no priest needed         |
| **Equality**            | No caste, no gender discrimination before God           |
| **Vernacular language** | Songs/poetry in local languages (Hindi, Marathi, Tamil) |
| **Simple devotion**     | Singing, dancing, chanting — complex rituals nahi       |
| **Social reform**       | Against caste discrimination and untouchability         |
| **Nirguna vs Saguna**   | Nirguna = formless God (Kabir)                          | Saguna = God with form (Tulsidas) |
| **Guru importance**     | Personal spiritual teacher essential                    |

### Major Bhakti Saints — MUST REMEMBER:

| Saint          | Period       | Region      | Language         | God/Deity        | Key Contribution                    |
| -------------- | ------------ | ----------- | ---------------- | ---------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Ramanuja**   | 11th-12th c. | South India | Tamil/Sanskrit   | Vishnu           | Vishishtadvaita philosophy          |
| **Basavanna**  | 12th c.      | Karnataka   | Kannada          | Shiva            | Lingayat movement                   |
| **Ramananda**  | 14th c.      | North India | Hindi            | Rama             | Broke caste in Bhakti               |
| **Kabir Das**  | 15th c.      | Varanasi    | Hindi            | Formless God     | Hindu-Muslim unity, against rituals |
| **Mirabai**    | 15th-16th c. | Rajasthan   | Hindi/Rajasthani | Krishna          | Women's bhakti, defied society      |
| **Tulsidas**   | 16th c.      | UP          | Awadhi Hindi     | Rama             | Ramcharitmanas                      |
| **Surdas**     | 16th c.      | UP/Agra     | Braj Bhasha      | Krishna          | Sur Sagar (krishna bhajans)         |
| **Chaitanya**  | 15th-16th c. | Bengal      | Bengali          | Krishna          | Kirtan tradition                    |
| **Tukaram**    | 17th c.      | Maharashtra | Marathi          | Vitthal (Vishnu) | Abhangas (devotional poems)         |
| **Guru Nanak** | 15th-16th c. | Punjab      | Punjabi          | Formless God     | Founded Sikhism                     |
| **Andal**      | 8th c.       | Tamil Nadu  | Tamil            | Vishnu           | First woman Bhakti saint            |

### Nirguna vs Saguna Bhakti:

| Aspect      | Nirguna Bhakti                        | Saguna Bhakti                        |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| God concept | Formless, attributeless God           | God with form and qualities          |
| Key saints  | Kabir, Guru Nanak, Namdev             | Tulsidas, Surdas, Mirabai, Chaitanya |
| Rituals     | Mostly rejected                       | Accepted with simplicity             |
| Focus       | Knowledge + devotion                  | Pure emotional devotion              |
| Examples    | Kabir's dohas — "na mandir na masjid" | Tulsidas's Ram as ideal king         |

### Impact of Bhakti Movement:

1. **Social reform** — challenged untouchability (Kabir was a weaver, Raidas was a cobbler!)
2. **Language development** — Hindi, Marathi, Bengali literatures grew
3. **Religious harmony** — Hindu-Muslim tensions reduced (Kabir, Guru Nanak)
4. **Women empowerment** — Mirabai, Andal showed women can be spiritual leaders
5. **Sikhism origin** — Guru Nanak's Bhakti evolved into a new religion
6. **Music tradition** — Kirtan, bhajan traditions developed

## 📌 2. SUFI MOVEMENT — Islam ki Mystical Tradition

> 🟡 Tier B | Frequency: 2/4 | Section: B + C

### Definition:

**Sufism** Islam ki mystical tradition hai jisme **direct personal experience of God** (Allah) ko emphasize kiya jaata hai — instead of just following laws. "Sufi" word **"suf"** (wool) se aaya — sufis simple woolen clothes pehente the as a sign of simplicity.

### Key Principles of Sufism:

| Principle   | Meaning                                          |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Tawhid**  | Oneness of God — everything is God               |
| **Fana**    | Annihilation of ego — becoming one with God      |
| **Ishq**    | Divine love — loving God passionately            |
| **Silsila** | Chain of masters — teacher to student lineage    |
| **Khanqah** | Sufi hospice/center where teachings happen       |
| **Sama**    | Devotional music, qawwali — music as path to God |
| **Zikr**    | Repetition of God's name in meditation           |

### Major Sufi Orders (Silsilas) in India:

| Silsila        | Founded by          | Area            | Famous Saint                   |
| -------------- | ------------------- | --------------- | ------------------------------ |
| **Chishti**    | Moinuddin Chishti   | Rajasthan/Delhi | Nizamuddin Auliya, Amir Khusro |
| **Suhrawardi** | Bahauddin Zakariya  | Multan/Punjab   | Bahauddin Zakariya             |
| **Qadri**      | Abdul Qadir Gilani  | South India     | —                              |
| **Naqshbandi** | Bahauddin Naqshband | North India     | Ahmad Sirhindi                 |

### Key Sufi Saints of India:

| Saint                 | Order   | Period       | Known For                                        |
| --------------------- | ------- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------ |
| **Moinuddin Chishti** | Chishti | 12th c.      | Ajmer Sharif dargah, came with Muhammad Ghori    |

### Bhakti vs Sufi Movement — Comparison:

| Feature         | Bhakti Movement                               | Sufi Movement             |
| --------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Religion        | Hindu                                         | Islamic                   |
| Core idea       | Personal devotion to God                      | Mystical union with Allah |
| Language        | Vernacular (Hindi, Marathi)                   | Persian, Urdu, local      |
| Music           | Bhajan, Kirtan                                | Qawwali, Sama             |
| Social reform   | Against caste                                 | Against rigid Islamic law |
| Common ground   | Both: love for God, equality, against rituals |
| Combined impact | Hindu-Muslim cultural synthesis               |

## 📌 3. BUDDHISM — The Middle Path

> 🟡 Tier B | Frequency: 2/4 | Section: A + B + C

### Definition:

**Buddhism** was founded by **Siddhartha Gautama** (563–483 BCE) in present-day Nepal/India. After years of meditation under a Bodhi tree at **Bodh Gaya**, he attained **enlightenment (Nirvana)** and became the **Buddha** (the Awakened One).

### 4 Noble Truths (Chaar Arya Satya) — MUST REMEMBER:

| #   | Noble Truth  | Meaning                                             |
| --- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Dukkha**   | Life is suffering — birth, old age, death, desire   |
| 2   | **Samudaya** | Suffering has a cause — it's desire/craving (tanha) |
| 3   | **Nirodha**  | Suffering can end — eliminate desire                |
| 4   | **Magga**    | The path to end suffering — the 8-fold path         |

### 8-Fold Path (Ashtangika Marga):

1. Right View
2. Right Intention
3. Right Speech
4. Right Action
5. Right Livelihood
6. Right Effort
7. Right Mindfulness
8. Right Concentration

### Key Teachings of Buddhism:

- **Ahimsa** — non-violence
- **Anatta** — no permanent self/soul
- **Anicca** — impermanence — everything changes
- **Karma** — actions have consequences
- **Nirvana** — liberation from cycle of rebirth
- **Middle Path** — neither extreme luxury nor extreme austerity

### Buddhism ka Decline in India (Kaise hua?) — 23-24 mein poocha:

1. **Brahmanical revival** — Adi Shankaracharya's Advaita defeated Buddhism in debates
2. **Bhakti movement** — made Hinduism accessible to all, reducing Buddhism's advantage
3. **Loss of royal patronage** — Gupta kings were Hindu
4. **Muslim invasions** — Nalanda, Taxila (Buddhist centers) destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khilji (1203)
5. **Buddha absorbed** — Hindus declared Buddha as Vishnu's avatar!
6. **Sangha corruption** — monasteries became corrupt over time

### Types of Buddhism:

| Type                     | Name              | Region                       | Belief                                |
| ------------------------ | ----------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Hinayana** (Theravada) | "Small Vehicle"   | Sri Lanka, Thailand, Myanmar | Original Buddha teachings, strict     |
| **Mahayana**             | "Large Vehicle"   | China, Japan, Korea          | Buddha as divine, bodhisattva concept |
| **Vajrayana**            | "Diamond Vehicle" | Tibet, Mongolia              | Tantric Buddhism                      |

## 📌 4. JAINISM — The Path of Non-Violence

> 🔴 Tier A | Frequency: 3/4 | Section: A + B + C

### Definition:

**Jainism** is one of India's oldest religions, founded/revived by **Mahavira** (599–527 BCE) — the 24th **Tirthankara** (ford-maker). "Jina" means "conqueror" — one who conquers inner enemies (desires, ego). Jainism emphasizes **extreme non-violence and self-discipline**.

### 5 Great Vows (Pancha Mahavrata) of Jainism:

| #   | Vow              | Meaning                                     |
| --- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Ahimsa**       | Non-violence — not harming any living being |
| 2   | **Satya**        | Truth — always speak truth                  |
| 3   | **Asteya**       | Non-stealing — don't take what's not given  |
| 4   | **Brahmacharya** | Celibacy — control of senses                |
| 5   | **Aparigraha**   | Non-possession — limit material possessions |

### How is Jain Literature Classified? — EXAM FAVORITE:

**Jain canonical literature is called "Agamas":**

```
JAIN LITERATURE
├── AGAMAS (Canonical texts)
│   ├── 12 Angas (main texts) — 11 survive, 1 lost
│   ├── 12 Upangas (secondary)
│   ├── 10 Prakirnas (miscellaneous)
│   ├── 6 Chhedasutras (discipline)
│   ├── 4 Mulasutras (basic)
│   └── 2 Culika Sutras
├── Language: Ardha-Magadhi (Agamas), Prakrit, Sanskrit
└── Non-canonical literature (commentaries, stories)
```

**Two Sects and their texts:**
| Sect | Texts | Language |
|------|-------|---------|
| **Digambara** | Reject original Agamas as lost; use Shatkhandagama | Prakrit/Sanskrit |
| **Shvetambara** | Accept Agamas | Ardha-Magadhi |

### Key Concepts of Jainism:

| Concept          | Meaning                                       |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Anekantavada** | Many-sidedness of truth — no absolute truth   |
| **Syadvada**     | Conditional predication — "maybe" philosophy  |
| **Karma**        | Matter that attaches to soul based on actions |
| **Moksha**       | Liberation when soul is free from karma       |
| **Tirthankara**  | 24 spiritual teachers who show path           |
| **Kevala Jnana** | Omniscience — perfect knowledge               |

### Jainism's Impact on Society:

| Area                | Impact                                            |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Non-violence**    | Influenced Gandhi's satyagraha movement           |
| **Vegetarianism**   | Strict vegetarian diet became widespread in India |
| **Business ethics** | Jain merchants known for ethical business         |
| **Architecture**    | Dilwara temples (Mt. Abu) — masterpiece in marble |
| **Logic**           | Anekantavada influenced Indian logic tradition    |
| **Animal welfare**  | First systematic concern for all life forms       |

### Two Sects of Jainism:

| Feature            | Digambara                   | Shvetambara              |
| ------------------ | --------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Meaning            | "Sky-clad" (naked)          | "White-clad"             |
| Monks' dress       | No clothes                  | White robes              |
| Women's liberation | Women cannot achieve moksha | Women can achieve moksha |
| Texts              | Different canon             | Accept Agamas            |
| Location           | South India mainly          | North/West India mainly  |

## 📌 5. SHAD-DARSHANA — 6 Orthodox Systems of Indian Philosophy

> 🟡 Tier B | Frequency: 2/4 | Section: A + C

### Definition:

**Shad-Darshana** ("6 views/systems") are the **6 orthodox (Astika) schools** of Indian philosophy that accept the authority of the Vedas. "Darshana" means "vision" or "way of seeing" — each system has a unique way of understanding reality.

### 6 Systems — MUST REMEMBER (Pair them up!):

| #   | System                       | Founder    | Key Text           | Core Idea                                             |
| --- | ---------------------------- | ---------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Nyaya**                    | Gautama    | Nyaya Sutras       | Logic and epistemology — how do we know?              |
| 2   | **Vaisheshika**              | Kanada     | Vaisheshika Sutras | Atomic theory — world made of atoms                   |
| 3   | **Sankhya**                  | Kapila     | Sankhya Karika     | Dualism — Purusha (consciousness) + Prakriti (matter) |
| 4   | **Yoga**                     | Patanjali  | Yoga Sutras        | Control of mind leads to liberation                   |
| 5   | **Purva Mimamsa**            | Jaimini    | Mimamsa Sutras     | Vedic rituals are the highest path                    |
| 6   | **Vedanta (Uttara Mimamsa)** | Badarayana | Brahma Sutras      | Brahman = ultimate reality (Upanishads-based)         |

### Memory Trick:

**"NY-SA-YO-PU-VE"** or pair them: **Nyaya-Vaisheshika** | **Sankhya-Yoga** | **Mimamsa-Vedanta**

### Brief on Each System:

**1. Nyaya** — "Logic School"

- Focus: How to gain valid knowledge
- 4 means of knowledge: Perception, Inference, Comparison, Testimony
- Helped develop Indian logic and debate tradition

**2. Vaisheshika** — "Atomism School"

- World = combinations of eternal atoms
- Kanada said matter is made of **paramanu** (atoms) — 2500 years before Dalton!
- 7 categories of reality

**3. Sankhya** — "Enumeration School"

- **25 tattvas** (elements) of reality
- **Purusha** (soul/consciousness) + **Prakriti** (nature/matter)
- Prakriti has 3 gunas: Sattva (goodness), Rajas (passion), Tamas (inertia)

**4. Yoga** — "Union School"

- Patanjali's **Ashtanga Yoga** (8 limbs): Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi
- Mind control → liberation
- World's oldest systematic psychology!

**5. Purva Mimamsa** — "Earlier Inquiry"

- Vedic rituals (yagna) are the supreme path
- Dharma = following Vedic injunctions
- Focus: karma-kanda (action portion of Vedas)

**6. Vedanta** — "End of Vedas"

- Based on Upanishads, Brahma Sutras, Bhagavad Gita
- 3 main sub-schools:
  - **Advaita** (Shankaracharya): Brahman alone is real, world is Maya
  - **Vishishtadvaita** (Ramanuja): God + souls + world all real but God supreme
  - **Dvaita** (Madhvacharya): God and soul are eternally different

## 📌 6. RAMAKRISHNA MISSION & SOCIO-RELIGIOUS REFORM

> 🟡 Tier B | Frequency: 2/4 | Section: B + C

### Ramakrishna Mission:

- Founded by **Swami Vivekananda** in **1897** in memory of his guru **Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa**
- Headquarters: **Belur Math**, West Bengal

### Key Features:

| Feature                        | Detail                              |
| ------------------------------ | ----------------------------------- |
| **"Manav seva = Madhav seva"** | Serving humans = Serving God        |
| **Practical Vedanta**          | Apply philosophy in real life       |
| **Universal religion**         | "All religions lead to same truth"  |
| **Social service**             | Schools, hospitals, disaster relief |
| **Women education**            | Vivekananda strongly supported      |

### Swami Vivekananda's Contribution:

- **1893 Chicago Speech** — represented Hinduism at World Parliament of Religions
- Said: "Sisters and Brothers of America" — audience gave standing ovation
- Spread **Vedanta** to the West
- Founded Ramakrishna Mission for social service

### Other 19th Century Reform Movements:

| Movement                 | Founder             | Year | Key Reform                                      |
| ------------------------ | ------------------- | ---- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Brahmo Samaj**         | Raja Ram Mohan Roy  | 1828 | Against sati, child marriage, polytheism        |
| **Prarthana Samaj**      | M.G. Ranade         | 1867 | Maharashtra — widow remarriage, women education |
| **Arya Samaj**           | Dayananda Saraswati | 1875 | "Back to Vedas", against idol worship, caste    |
| **Ramakrishna Mission**  | Vivekananda         | 1897 | Practical Vedanta, social service               |
| **Theosophical Society** | H.P. Blavatsky      | 1875 | Hindu-Buddhist philosophy to West               |
| **Satya Shodhak Samaj**  | Jyotirao Phule      | 1873 | Maharashtra, anti-Brahmin, lower caste rights   |

### Satya Shodhak Samaj (Truth-Seeking Society):

- Founded by **Jyotirao Phule** in **1873** in Pune
- For **OBCs, lower castes, women**
- Against Brahmin monopoly on religion and education
- His wife **Savitribai Phule** — India's first woman teacher
- Book: **"Gulamgiri" (Slavery)** — compared caste oppression to American slavery
